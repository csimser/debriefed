Marine Counterintelligence Specialists operate at the intersection of human intelligence, threat assessment, and national security. You've conducted CI investigations, run HUMINT sources, assessed insider threats, and operated in some of the most sensitive intelligence environments the Marine Corps supports.

That experience is rare. The civilian market for it — in the IC, in federal law enforcement, and in corporate security — is strong and well-compensated.

## Your Clearance Is Your Foundation

0211 Marines typically hold TS/SCI clearances, often with polygraph. This is your most valuable asset in the civilian market:

- TS/SCI: +$40K–$70K salary premium vs. uncleared equivalent
- TS/SCI + CI Poly: +$55K–$85K premium
- TS/SCI + FS Poly: +$70K–$110K premium

Protect it. Don't let it lapse. Don't do anything that jeopardizes it.

- Insider Threat Analyst — IC and major federal agencies

**Federal Law Enforcement:**
- FBI Special Agent — Counterintelligence Division (most direct path for 0211 background)
- DSS (Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency) — civilian CI investigator
- DHS/OIG investigators — fraud and insider threat
- NCIS Civilian Special Agent

**Defense Contracting:**
- CI Support Analyst — supporting IC and DoD contracts
- Program Security Officer (PSO) — protecting DoD classified programs
- Personnel Security Specialist — JPAS/DISS management
- Insider Threat Program Manager — high-growth specialty

**Corporate Security:**
- Corporate Intelligence Analyst — threat assessment for large organizations
- Insider Threat Program Lead — large tech, defense, financial firms
- Security Investigations Manager — employee misconduct, IP theft

## FBI Counterintelligence: Your Best Federal Path

The FBI's Counterintelligence Division is the most direct match for 0211 experience. Marine CI background is explicitly valued in their hiring:

**Requirements:**
- Bachelor's degree (any field)
- Under 37 years old at time of appointment (waiver possible with military service)
- Polygraph, medical, and fitness testing

**0211 advantages in FBI hiring:**
- CI investigation experience directly matches FBI CI Division mission
- Source handling experience is rare and valued
- Existing clearance shortens background investigation timeline
- Marine Corps reputation helps in final hiring decisions

**Starting grade:** GS-10; journeyman at GS-13 ($100K–$130K depending on locality)

## DCSA (Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency)

DCSA is the most direct federal employer for 0211 Marines. As the DoD's primary CI and security agency, DCSA hires CI agents, industrial security specialists, and threat analysts.

**Roles:**
- **Special Agent (CI)** — field investigations, source operations, threat assessment
- **Industrial Security Specialist** — protecting classified defense programs
- **Insider Threat Analyst** — monitoring classified program personnel

GS-7 to GS-12 entry depending on degree and experience. Strong veteran hiring pipeline.

## Defense Contracting: Program Security Officer Track

Program Security Officers (PSOs) protect classified defense programs — SAPs, SARs, and conventional classified programs. It's one of the highest-paying non-technical security roles in contracting:

**What PSOs do:**
- Manage personnel security requirements for classified programs
- Investigate security incidents and violations
- Interface with government security officials
- Oversee physical security, OPSEC, and CI support

**Salary:** $90K–$130K; senior PSOs and Special Program Security Officers (SPSOs) earn $130K–$175K

**Target employers:**
- Northrop Grumman — largest SAP contractor
- Lockheed Martin — heavy classified program portfolio
- Raytheon / RTX — defense systems programs
- L3Harris — communications and intelligence systems
- Booz Allen Hamilton — IC and DoD program support

**Certification:** **SAPCO** (Special Access Program Control Officer) training is preferred; **FSO** (Facility Security Officer) certification via NCMS is the primary path.

## Corporate Intelligence and Insider Threat

Large technology companies, financial institutions, and defense contractors run internal intelligence programs. Your 0211 background maps directly:

**Corporate counterintelligence:**
- Monitor for foreign intelligence service targeting of employees
- Investigate IP theft and economic espionage
- Assess insider threats before they become incidents
- Brief leadership on threat environment

**Who hires for this:**
- Amazon, Google, Microsoft — large internal security intelligence teams
- Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan — financial intelligence and investigations
- Lockheed, Boeing, Raytheon — cleared industrial security programs
- Large pharmaceutical companies — IP protection

Salaries range $95K–$145K for experienced CI professionals in corporate environments.

## Certifications and Credentials

Your clearance and experience are your primary credentials. Add:

- **CFE** (Certified Fraud Examiner) — valuable for investigations-focused track
- **CPP** (Certified Protection Professional) — corporate security leadership
- **PMP** — if targeting program security officer or security program management roles
- **CompTIA Security+** — opens DoD IT security roles as a side path

No certification replaces your operational CI experience. They supplement and formalize it.
