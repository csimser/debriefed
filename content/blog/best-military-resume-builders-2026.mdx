## Free Tools

### Resume Engine (transition.mil)

Resume Engine is the DoD's official free resume builder. It's built into the Transition Assistance Program and requires no signup.

**What it does well:** Structured format, DoD familiarity, no cost, no friction. If you want to produce a basic civilian resume quickly and aren't ready to think about optimization, Resume Engine is a functional starting point.

**Limitations:** The translation is template-based, not AI-assisted. You get civilian job title suggestions, but the tool won't help you reframe your bullet points or tailor your resume to a specific job posting. No cover letter generation, no job matching.

**Best for:** E-1 through E-5 veterans who need a basic resume fast and don't have complex experience to translate.

**Used with Debriefed:** Resume Engine gets you started. Debriefed takes your bullet points and refines them for specific civilian job postings.

### Hire Heroes USA

Hire Heroes USA is a nonprofit that provides free resume coaching, job search assistance, and career counseling to veterans and military spouses. It's staffed by human coaches, not algorithms.

**What it does well:** Real human expertise. A Hire Heroes coach can ask you questions that surface experience you might not think to include, identify civilian career paths you haven't considered, and review your final resume. For veterans with complex or unusual career paths, this is genuinely valuable.

**Limitations:** Wait times. Hire Heroes is in demand and coaching appointments aren't instant. The process takes days to weeks, not minutes. There's also no AI-assisted document generation — everything is human-paced.

**Best for:** Veterans who want career guidance alongside resume help, or those with 15+ years of complex service that needs a human to untangle.

**Used with Debriefed:** Use Hire Heroes for career strategy and coaching. Use Debriefed to produce the actual documents quickly and iterate on them.

### O*NET Military Crosswalk (onetonline.org)

O*NET is the Department of Labor's occupational database, and it includes a military crosswalk that maps MOS/rating/AFSC codes to civilian SOC occupation codes. It's a lookup tool, not a resume builder.

**What it does well:** Authoritative civilian occupation mapping. If you want to understand what civilian job categories your MOS maps to — with salary data and growth projections — O*NET is the source of truth.

**Limitations:** It's a reference tool, not a document builder. You look up your MOS, see the related civilian occupations, and then you're on your own to write the resume.

**Best for:** Research phase of transition. Use it to understand your civilian career options before building your resume.

---

### USAJOBS Resume Builder

USAJOBS has a built-in resume builder specifically for federal job applications. It captures the required fields for federal hiring: hours per week, supervisor contact information, salary history, and the detailed experience narratives that federal hiring managers need.

**What it does well:** Federal format compliance. If your target is a GS-series government job, the USAJOBS builder ensures your resume meets the minimum requirements. It's free and the output is directly uploadable to federal applications.

**Limitations:** It only produces federal resumes. It doesn't help you translate military experience into civilian language — you still have to write the bullet points yourself. No AI assistance, no cover letters, no job matching.

**Best for:** Veterans exclusively targeting federal employment who want a dedicated federal resume tool.

**Used with Debriefed:** Debriefed generates your federal resume format. Use USAJOBS builder to upload it directly to applications.

### Teal (tealhq.com)

Teal is a general-purpose AI resume builder with strong ATS optimization features. It's not military-specific but has a large user base and polished product.

**What it does well:** ATS keyword optimization, job tracker, resume scoring against specific job postings. If you're targeting private sector roles and your military experience is straightforward to translate, Teal is a capable tool.

**Limitations:** No military context whatsoever. Teal doesn't know what a 68W is, what NCOES means, or how to translate clearance-related experience. You'd be doing all the translation work yourself.

**Best for:** Veterans whose civilian career path is clear and who need ATS optimization more than military translation.

---

### Rezi (rezi.ai)

Rezi focuses on ATS score optimization with real-time feedback as you write. It scores your resume against job descriptions and suggests improvements.

**What it does well:** ATS compliance and keyword matching are Rezi's core strengths. The real-time feedback loop is useful for iterating on resume content.

**Limitations:** Like Teal, zero military context. Rezi is a polishing tool, not a translation tool.

**Best for:** Veterans who have already translated their military experience and want ATS optimization on the output.

## Professional Resume Writing Services

### CareerProPlus

CareerProPlus is one of the longest-running military resume writing services, with writers who specialize in senior NCO, officer, and executive-level transitions. Packages range from $249 to $1,000+.

**What it does well:** Human expertise for complex transitions. Senior military professionals with 20+ years of service, multiple commands, and executive-level experience often benefit from a writer who can conduct a career interview and structure a narrative that captures their full scope of impact.

**Limitations:** Price and time. $600+ for a resume package is a significant investment, and the turnaround is 3-10 business days. You typically get 1-2 revision rounds included.

**Best for:** E-9s, O-5+, and senior NCOs targeting executive or senior civilian roles where a $600 investment represents a small fraction of the salary upside.

---

### Empire Resume

Empire Resume offers military-specific resume writing at a lower price point than CareerProPlus ($249-$349). Writers have military backgrounds.

**What it does well:** More accessible price point than the premium services. Military-background writers understand the context.

**Limitations:** Lower price point typically means less time per resume and fewer revision rounds. The federal resume option isn't as strong as CareerProPlus.

**Best for:** Mid-career veterans (E-6 through O-3) who want human writing assistance without the premium service price.
