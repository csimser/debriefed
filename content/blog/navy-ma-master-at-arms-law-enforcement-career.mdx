## Federal Law Enforcement: Your Best First Move

Navy MAs are among the most competitive candidates for federal law enforcement hiring because you already have verified security clearances, documented law enforcement experience, and familiarity with federal standards.

**Top federal LE agencies recruiting MAs:**

**NCIS (Naval Criminal Investigative Service):**
The most direct pipeline for Navy MAs. NCIS actively recruits from the MA rating — you already know their referral process from the other side of the report. Special Agent positions start at GS-7/9 and promote to GS-13 journeyman. Starting salary ranges from $82K–$95K in most locations.

**CBP (Customs and Border Protection):**
One of the largest federal LE employers. CBP Officers and Border Patrol Agents start at GL-5/7/9 depending on education. With veterans' preference, Navy MAs are competitive at GL-7 or above.

**NCUA, USMS, ATF, DEA, Secret Service:**
All have veteran hiring programs and value law enforcement experience. Processing timelines range from 6–18 months — apply early.

**DoD Civilian Police (GS-0083):**
The most immediate option — civilian police officers on military installations. Work in the same environment you trained in, with full federal benefits, and competitive GS-5 to GS-9 entry.

## Anti-Terrorism Experience: A Premium Skill

If you held AT/FP (Anti-Terrorism/Force Protection) duties, you have skills that translate directly to high-paying private sector roles:

- **Corporate threat assessment** — identifying vulnerabilities in facilities, operations, or executive schedules
- **Event security management** — large-scale event planning and security operations
- **Travel security consulting** — global risk assessment for corporate travelers
- **Security program auditing** — assessing other organizations' security postures

Employers in banking, energy, and technology pay $90K–$130K+ for security professionals with AT/FP backgrounds.

## The Corporate Security Track

For MAs who want to avoid shift work, the corporate security path leads to the best long-term outcomes:

**Track:**
1. **Security Analyst / Operations Specialist** ($70K–$85K) — typically 1–2 years
2. **Security Manager** ($85K–$110K) — regional or departmental ownership
3. **Director of Corporate Security** ($120K–$170K) — program ownership

**Certifications that accelerate this track:**
- **CPP** (Certified Protection Professional) — ASIS International; gold standard; $15K+ salary premium
- **PSP** (Physical Security Professional) — systems and technology focus
- **PCI** (Professional Certified Investigator) — if investigations are your focus

**Industries with the strongest security budgets:**
- Financial services
- Technology (Amazon, Google, Meta all have large security organizations)
- Energy sector (pipeline, offshore, refinery security)
- Pharmaceutical (IP protection, executive security)

## The Investigation Track

If your MA service included significant investigative work — NCIS liaison, sexual assault response, drug investigations — you have a path into:

- **Private investigations** — licensed PI work in insurance fraud, corporate investigations
- **Corporate investigations** — internal fraud, employee misconduct
- **Insurance special investigations** (SIU) — high demand, nationally available
- **Federal agencies** — NCIS Special Agent being the most direct

For the NCIS Special Agent track specifically:
- GS-7 entry ($57K–$75K depending on locality)
- Promotes to GS-13 journeyman within 4–5 years ($100K–$130K)
- Requires a bachelor's degree for most entry points
- Navy background is a direct advantage in the background investigation

## Federal Resume for GS-0083 (DoD Civilian Police)

If you're targeting civilian police positions on military installations, your federal resume needs to highlight:

- Number of patrols conducted
- Types of incidents handled (with civilian terminology)
- Arrests made / cases processed
- Firearms qualifications and use-of-force training
- Supervisory experience if applicable
- Any specialized training (K9, waterborne, AT/FP)

DoD civilian police positions are GS-5 to GS-9 at entry, with promotion to GS-12 as a senior officer or supervisor. The familiar environment can ease the transition while you build civilian credentials.

## Your 90-Day Action Plan

1. **Apply to USAJOBS for GS-0083** in your target location — DoD civilian police posts are open most of the year and are among the fastest-hiring federal positions
2. **Contact NCIS recruiting** directly if you have investigations experience — they have a Navy MA pipeline and respond to direct outreach
3. **Begin CPP exam preparation** — 3–4 months of study; opens corporate security director track significantly
4. **Register on ClearanceJobs.com** with your clearance level — cleared security professionals get recruiter outreach quickly
5. **Run your performance evaluations through Debriefed** — translate NCIS, AT/FP, DD-1408, and Navy-specific terminology into civilian law enforcement and security language
